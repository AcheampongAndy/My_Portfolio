Part II - (Effects of Ford GoBike System Data Exploration Characteristics on Their Duration Seconds)¶

by (Acheampong Andrews)¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the characteristics of GoBike that could be used to predict their duration seconds. The main focus was on the three variables of GoBike: bike_id, member_gender, and bike_share_for_all_trip.

Dataset Overview¶

This data set includes information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area.

The start_time and end_time were object data type, I changed them to datetime data type and split the date and time into four variables start_time, end_time, start_date, and end_date. I also remove all the NAN values to make the dataset ready for analysis.

Distribution of GoBike Duration Seconds¶

Duration second has a long-tailed distribution, with a lot of small time end, and few on the greatest time end. When plotted on a log-scale, the duration seconds distribution looks a normal distribution, with the peak between 20000 and 60000, and its slightly skewed to the right.
